Tea-ching us about Multiculturalism in Singapore

Scope

  • Tea as a representation of different cultures in society
  • Understanding the similarities and differences between different teas and how that relates to Singapore's racial harmony

Chinese Tea

  • Migrants bringing their cultural traditions with them, including tea drinking ceremonies
  • Bak Kut Teh as a distinctly Singaporean dish

  • Indians with their Masala Chai, and Malays with their Teh Tarik

Talking about Tea

  • Teh Susu vs Teh Si?
  • Referring to the same tea in different ways

  • What about Teh Tarik? Taking a closer look....

Cultural Changes

  • Given Singapore's diverse mix of cultures, one needs to be sensitive to the tastes of others
  • Especially so for Hawkers who make their living off understanding their customer's preferences
  • Each tea has distinct character and identity, but have absorbed influences from other teas

Case Study: Indian Teas

  • Local Flavors vs Traditional Spice Teas?
  • Singaporean variants: Teh Chino, Teh Halia etc.
  • Traditionally not pulled. The "tarik" component was borrowed from Malay/Indonesian Teas.

"Indian" Teh Tarik

  • Teas of different cultures cross paths at different points in time
    • The availability of both indian tea leaves and the common tarik techniques
  • And developing beyond their original tastes
  • While retaining the original flavors that make them unique
    • Indian teas, although pulled and of a variety of flavors now, are still made with the same leaves

Despite having Hokkien and Malay elements in it's name, Teh Tarik is most commonly prepared by Indians in Singapore.

Moving forward

The teas enjoyed by Singaporeans today are the result of constant iteration and reinvention over the years.

Perhaps what made Singapore unique was this indifference towards a food/beverage item, which translates to how people treat each other.

Given the greater influx of migrant workers today, such a mentality is all the more important.

We should be open to learning from our international peers, and integrating useful traits to aid in our personal growth so as to emerge a strong and more well-rounded individual.
